## Summary

BlackRock — the world's largest asset manager — officially downgraded U.S. equities to "neutral" on March 23, 2026. Wei Li (Global Chief Investment Strategist) and Rob Kapito (President) both warn that equity markets are severely mispricing the energy risk from the Iran conflict.

## Key Points from Wei Li

- BlackRock has **"flattened equity directional exposure to neutral"** — moved from overweight to neutral
- Pivoting toward **thematic investing** rather than broad market directional bets
- Equities are not properly pricing in the energy disruption risk

## Key Points from Rob Kapito (President)

- **GDP impact:** Up to 2 percentage points of growth could be lost, even if the conflict ends soon
- **Inflation impact:** Similar 2-point spike expected, regardless of war duration
- **Oil price warning:** Could still spike to **$150/barrel** even "if we announce tomorrow the war is over" — supply chains take time to recover
- **Historical playbook:** "When there was a conflict like this, you bought short-term Treasuries, you bought gold, and you shorted the equity market"
- Markets are showing an **uneven response** — too complacent about the economic fallout

## The Macro Picture

- Iran conflict → disrupted energy supply → oil price spike → inflation → Fed can't cut rates → equities under pressure
- Supply chain disruptions will linger even after any ceasefire
- Broader concern: shipping routes, energy infrastructure, global supply chains all at risk
- This is not just an energy sector problem — it ripples across the entire economy
